Causes of Water Pollution

Industrial Waste

Factories and industries discharge harmful chemicals and pollutants into rivers, lakes, and oceans, causing severe contamination of water sources.

Agricultural Runoff

Pesticides, fertilizers, and waste from farms flow into water bodies, contributing to eutrophication and other harmful effects on aquatic life.

Deforestation

Clear-cutting forests leads to soil erosion, and the runoff carries silt and debris into water bodies, impacting water quality and ecosystems.

Urbanization

Rapid urban growth results in increased sewage, garbage, and industrial waste being dumped into water bodies, increasing pollution levels.

Oil Spills

Accidental spills of oil and petroleum products into oceans and rivers severely damage aquatic life and affect water quality for years.

Plastic Pollution

Plastic waste, which takes hundreds of years to decompose, clogs waterways and harms marine life, leading to long-lasting pollution.

Climate Change

Changing weather patterns due to climate change increase the frequency and intensity of floods, leading to the spread of waterborne diseases and pollutants.

Mining Activities

Mining operations introduce harmful chemicals such as mercury into water systems, degrading water quality and harming aquatic life.

Waste Disposal

Improper disposal of household, hospital, and industrial waste often leads to hazardous chemicals contaminating nearby water sources.

Wastewater Discharge

Untreated or poorly treated wastewater discharged into rivers and lakes can introduce harmful pathogens and chemicals, polluting the water.
